Copyright Law Basics

Copyright laws are federal laws put in place after 1978. Everything on the internet should be assumed to be copyrighted unless it says other wise. Violation of copyright laws results in a law suit in the federal court. Being said that it is a federal law, if content is copied outside of the United States, it is hard to enforce these laws.

Fair Use Laws

There are some fair use limitations on copy right laws. This allows for use of others' work without approval from the owner. This is only allowed for ceterain uses. For example for educationals or scholarship purposes. Only a small portion of material can be use with the indication of the owner or author.

Copy Right Damages

If you are liable for copyright violation, the copyright holder can choose their recovery. One being actual damage for example loss of profits. ANother is statutory damages. Those range from $750 to $30,000 for each infringing copy. The last way is if the copyright holder proves that the act was done willfully then the court can increase the statutory damages up to $150,000 for each copy.
